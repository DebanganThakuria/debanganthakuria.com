When a computer engineering student dies at his desk and wakes up in the body of Lucian Caelmont — the talentless, soul-damaged disgrace of a fallen noble house — he inherits a life nobody would envy. A broken soul. A family that's already written him off. And a teaching position at a prestigious magic academy, arranged through nepotism so transparent it's practically a public insult.

But Lucian sees what no mage in a thousand years has seen.

In a world where power is measured by the soul and advancement is bought with Soul Fragments harvested from deadly Spirit Marches, everyone obsesses over the same two variables: soul quality and cultivation technique. Nobody has ever asked the right question.

Nobody has ever thought about geometry.

Armed with an engineer's mind, a discovery that could shatter the foundations of magical theory, and a spell he built from first principles using hydrogen atoms — Lucian becomes something the world has no category for. A Third Circle professor with the mana regeneration of a god. A mind running ten parallel thoughts while pretending to struggle with one. A man who can dismantle your spell mid-flight and make it look like you simply missed.

There's nothing that can faze Professor Lucian. But people still call him a fraud.
