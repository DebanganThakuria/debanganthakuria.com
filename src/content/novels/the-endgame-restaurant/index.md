Devan is an unremarkable employee with one extraordinary habit: he has played *Worldroot Online* for fourteen years — through its golden age, its decline, its official ending, and its long afterlife as a tourist attraction where people wander the old zones on free level-1 accounts. The studio dissolved years ago. The servers were scheduled for shutdown.

Somehow, they never went dark. And nobody looks closely at a dead game.

Then the new generation of helmets ships: full gustatory rendering. Taste, in VR. And Kael realizes something no one else has — *Worldroot* has simulated flavor data for fourteen years that no human being has ever accessed. Every beast, every herb, every fruit in the game, never tasted by anyone. He will be the first person to ever taste a world.

He opens a small restaurant in the Undervault — one of the last dungeons that still generates monsters, far below the tourist zones, where level-1 accounts can't survive the walk. He hunts, butchers, experiments, cooks. It's the best hobby of his life.

Then he maxes Cooking — his final skill — and the game he thought he'd finished says:

> **Congratulations. You have completed the game.**
>
> **You may now accept guests from another world in your restaurant. Do you accept?**

The woman who steps through his door in a mining lamp and rock-dust cloak is not an NPC. The copper coins she pays with convert, impossibly, into real money in his bank account. The transfers come from a company that no longer exists.

Somewhere between worlds, a dead game's servers are still running, still updating, still making offers. And in a fantasy world he's never seen, doors with warm light under them have started appearing in deep places, and rumors are rising from the mines: *there is a room below the world where the food is a miracle, and the card in your pocket can take you home.*
