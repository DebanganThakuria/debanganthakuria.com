Devan is an unremarkable employee with one extraordinary habit: he has played *Worldroot Online* for fourteen years — through its golden age, its decline, its official ending, and its long afterlife as a tourist attraction where people wander the old zones on free Level 1 accounts. The studio dissolved years ago. The servers were scheduled for shutdown.

Somehow, they never went dark. And nobody looks closely at a dead game.

Then the new generation of helmets ships: full gustatory rendering. Taste, in VR. And Devan realizes something no one else has — *Worldroot* has simulated flavor data for fourteen years since its first launch. Every beast, every herb, every fruit in the game, never tasted by anyone. He will be the first person to ever taste a world.

He opens a small restaurant in the Undervault — one of the last dungeons that still generates monsters, far below the tourist zones, where Level 1 accounts can't survive the walk. He hunts, butchers, experiments, cooks. It's the best hobby of his life.

Then he maxes Cooking — his final skill — and the game he thought he'd finished says:

> **Congratulations. You have completed the game.**
>
> **You may now accept guests from another world in your restaurant. Do you accept?**

Somewhere between worlds, a dead game's servers are still running, still updating, still making offers. And in a fantasy world he's never seen, doors with warm light under them have started appearing in front of selected individuals.

"These endgame NPCs are very well-designed." Devan couldn't help but wonder as he sent his last customer out.
